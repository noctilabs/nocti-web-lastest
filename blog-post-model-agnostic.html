Model agnostic by design: why we don't lock clients into one LLM

Jun 10, 2026·5 min read

Every few months, a new model takes the lead on the benchmark that matters to you. Reasoning, cost, latency, a specific language, a specific domain: the ranking shifts constantly. Building an agent system tightly coupled to one provider means re-architecting every time that ranking shifts.

So we don't. The agents we build are designed around your workflows and your data, with the model treated as a swappable component behind that layer, not the foundation it's built on.

What this looks like in practice

In practice, it means the orchestration logic, the guardrails, and the integrations with your systems don't change when a model does. We can run a cheaper, faster model for high-volume, low-stakes steps, and a stronger one for the handful of decisions that need it, inside the same system.

Your agents shouldn't be hostage to one company's roadmap.

Why this matters for production

Production systems need to survive vendor outages, pricing changes, and deprecations. A model-agnostic architecture means a provider issue is a configuration change, not an emergency rebuild. It's a small design decision up front that saves months down the line.
